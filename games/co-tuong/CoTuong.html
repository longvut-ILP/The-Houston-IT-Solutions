Move Log (ICCS)

    How to Play Co Tướng (Xiangqi)

    Goal: checkmate the enemy General — leave it under attack with no legal escape.

    Making a move

    Click one of your pieces. Its legal moves appear as green dots (empty squares) and red rings (captures). Click a marker to move there, or click the piece again to deselect. Red moves first.

    The pieces

    • 帥/將 General — one step orthogonally, stays inside the 3×3 palace. The two Generals may never face each other down an open file.
    • 仕/士 Advisor — one step diagonally, inside the palace.
    • 相/象 Elephant — two points diagonally, can’t cross the river and is blocked if the midpoint is occupied.
    • 傌/馬 Horse — one straight then one diagonal (an “L”); blocked if a piece sits beside it in the straight direction.
    • 俥/車 Chariot — any distance in a straight line (like a rook).
    • 炮/砲 Cannon — moves like a chariot, but to capture it must jump exactly one piece (the “screen”).
    • 兵/卒 Soldier — one step forward; after crossing the river it may also step sideways. Never moves backward.

    Repetition (WXF rules)

    Endlessly repeating a position is judged: forcing a perpetual check or a perpetual chase of an unprotected piece loses for the offender. Other repetitions (idle shuffles, even exchanges) are a draw.

    Playing the Computer

    Tick “AI plays Black” to play against the engine, and pick a Difficulty. Untick it to play another person on the same screen.

    Office Tournament

    Type each person’s name and press Add (Enter works too). With two or more in line, press Start. The winner stays on, the loser goes to the back of the line, a draw keeps the champion, and colours swap each game. A 🔥 streak marks the King of the Hill. Press Add 🤖 Computer to drop the engine into the line as a contender. When a game ends, press Start next match ▶ to bring in the next challenger.
